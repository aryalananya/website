Nepali cuisine

Nepalese cuisine is a diverse, flavorful blend influenced by Indian and Tibetan traditions, characterized by staples like dal bhat (lentil soup and rice), steamed dumplings (momos), and tarkari (vegetable curry). Key ingredients include cumin, coriander, turmeric, garlic, ginger, and hot, tangy pickles (achaar). Regional specialties include dhido (thick porridge) in the mountains and Newari snacks in the Kathmandu Valley

Momo

Food is any nutritious substance—typically of plant, animal, or fungal origin—consumed by organisms to provide essential energy and nutrients for survival, growth, and bodily repair. It contains macronutrients (carbohydrates, fats, proteins) and micronutrients (vitamins, minerals) vital for sustaining life, maintaining health, and regulating bodily processes.

Momo are traditional Nepalese dumplings filled with minced meat (often buffalo, chicken, or pork) or vegetables, seasoned with fresh herbs and spices, and served steamed or fried with a savory tomato-based dipping sauce called achar. Originating from Tibet, they are a staple in Nepal and the Himalayas, commonly featuring round or half-moon shapes.
